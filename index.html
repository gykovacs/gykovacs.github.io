Introduction

I define myself as a data scientist grown up in the field of image processing. This site plays the role of an online CV and a place where some codes related to my research and hobby projects are made available for download.

Main Scientific Interests

A short overview of the fields I have research and professional experience in is given below with a reference to a selected paper in the topic:

  • Computer Science (computer vision, pattern recognition, HPC):
    • general aspects of digital signal processing and computer vision (Kovács, 2016)
    • digital geometry (Németh, Kovács, Fazekas, & Palágyi, 2016)
    • (bio)medical image analysis (Kovács & Hajdu, 2016)
    • nuclear image reconstruction (PET, CT) (Kis et al., 2011)
    • farm animal imaging (László et al., 2017)
    • human-computer interactions (Kovács, Makara, & Fazekas, 2009)
    • high-performance computing, parallel programming, distributed computing (Kovács, 2013)
  • Mathematics (machine learning):
    • classification and regression techniques (Kovács & Hajdu, 2013)
    • kernel methods (Kovács & Hajdu, 2013)
  • Physics (quantum mechanics):
    • theoretical solid state physics (Gulácsi, Kovács, & Gulácsi, 2014)
    • exact solutions of quantum systems (Gulácsi, Kovács, & Gulácsi, 2014)
  • Economy (computational finance):
    • modeling of financial processes
    • econometrics

Technical Skills

1M+ lines of code in C/C++; 100k+ lines of code in Java; 100k+ lines in bash; familiar with various SQL dialects, Matlab, R (including the package ggplot2 for scientific visualization), Scala and Python; solid knowledge of high performance computing in C/C++, including OpenMP, Pthreads, OpenMPI and OpenCL for GPU. Familiar with system administration and source code management (Linux, GNU make, cmake, qmake, automake). Working proficiency in typsetting by Latex.

Education

MSc. in Computer Science (2007), Applied Mathematics (2012), Physics (2017); PhD in Informatics (2016) each degree achieved at the University of Debrecen, Hungary.
